90-Day Implementation Roadmap
─────────────────────────────

## Guiding Principles
At 90 days, the organization should have eliminated the two highest-rework handoffs, established documented owners for every core handoff, and have at least one system-based workflow active in place of a manual email process.

This assessment is based on information provided in a single session. Findings should be validated with all relevant team leads before implementation.

## Month 1 — Stabilize
Target handoffs: Sales → R&D (Score: 1) and Quality → All Teams / CAPA (Score: 1).

• Deploy mandatory Sales brief intake form with required fields; Sales leadership to enforce before client brief is submitted.
• Publish R&D → Manufacturing scale-up checklist; no batch sheet accepted without completed checklist.
• Activate QMS CAPA tracking module; migrate all open CAPAs out of email into system by end of month.
• Define a 48-hour acknowledgment SLA for R&D upon brief receipt.

## Month 2 — Standardize
Target handoffs: Sales → Demand Planning (Score: 2), Demand Planning → Manufacturing (Score: 2), Demand Planning → Procurement (Score: 2).

• Migrate shared forecast to a versioned cloud file with edit history; publish weekly SLA for volume confirmation.
• Document standard operating procedures for all six assessed handoffs; assign a named function owner to each.
• Roll out a production schedule acknowledgment step — Manufacturing responds in writing to weekly schedule within 24 hours.

## Month 3 — Systematize
Target handoffs: Demand Planning → Procurement (ERP consolidation) and Manufacturing → Quality (EBR pilot).

• Scope and begin ERP lead time consolidation with Procurement and IT; target go-live in following quarter.
• Launch single-line electronic batch record pilot with Quality and Manufacturing; define success criteria.
• Evaluate PLM or project management tools for end-to-end job tracking from Sales brief to batch release.

## Responsible Functions
• Sales Leadership: brief template enforcement, forecast SLA compliance.
• R&D Management: checklist adoption, handoff documentation.
• Quality Assurance: QMS CAPA activation, EBR pilot lead.
• Demand Planning: forecast migration, schedule SLA ownership.
• Procurement / IT: ERP lead time consolidation.
• Operations / Manufacturing: schedule acknowledgment process, EBR participation.

## Success Metrics
• R&D brief-to-start lag time: target reduction from 3–5 days to under 1 day.
• R&D → Manufacturing first-pass rework rate: target reduction from ~30% to under 10%.
• Open CAPA average age: track and reduce month-over-month.
• Production schedule confirmation rate: % of schedules acknowledged in writing within 24 hours.
• Audit findings related to batch record completeness: target zero new findings by Day 90.
