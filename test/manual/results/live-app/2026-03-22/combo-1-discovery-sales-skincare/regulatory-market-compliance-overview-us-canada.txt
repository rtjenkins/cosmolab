Regulatory & Market Compliance Overview – US & Canada
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────

## United States (FDA – Cosmetic)
Product classified as a cosmetic under FDA regulations; claims (brightening, antioxidant, even skin tone) are permissible cosmetic claims.
Must comply with MoCRA (Modernization of Cosmetics Regulation Act) requirements including facility registration and product listing.
Labeling must meet FDA 21 CFR Part 701 requirements.

## Canada (Health Canada – Cosmetic)
Product must be notified to Health Canada within 10 days of first sale under the Cosmetic Regulations (Canada Consumer Product Safety Act).
Ingredient list must use INCI names; bilingual labeling (English and French) required.
Clean beauty ingredient standards should be cross-referenced against Health Canada's Cosmetic Ingredient Hotlist.

## Claims Compliance
Permitted claims: brightening, even skin tone, antioxidant protection — all cosmetic in nature.
No drug claims (e.g., "treats hyperpigmentation" or "clinically corrects") may be used in either market.
Claims substantiation documentation is recommended for retail and e-commerce listings.
